    5. User rights in relation to their personal data

    We are bound by the user rights granted by the GDPR provisions. Among them:

    Right of access (Art. 15 GDPR)

    The user has the right to receive or access data that were directly entered by him and also collected by the resource for additional use, as well as information about the collection’s goals, duration of data storage, data sources, and intended uses.

    Right to rectification (Art. 16 GDPR)

    The subject has the right to demand from the data controller that any inaccurate personal information about him be corrected without undue delay. The interested party will also have the right to complete any personal information that is missing, including using an additional declaration.

    Right to erasure (Art. 17 GDPR)

    The subject may request the deletion of data in the event of withdrawal of consent to their processing, in the event that the data is no longer needed for the purpose for which it was originally collected, but for some reason was not automatically deleted.

    Right to restriction of processing (Art. 18 GDPR)

    The subject has the right to demand to temporarily stop processing some data about him, but not delete them from the system. The reasons may be, for example, challenging the accuracy of the data, and the need to store the data for use by the subject in legal claims.

    Right to data portability (Art. 20 GDPR)

    If such a transfer is technically feasible, this is the right to receive personal data in a structured, machine-readable format for automatic transfer to another information controller without his personal involvement.
